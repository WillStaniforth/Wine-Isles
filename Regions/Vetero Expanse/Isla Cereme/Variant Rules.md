Some huge or larger creatures are so large, that regular weapons don't have as much effect without getting a really good hit in. This is represented in their stats as resistance to bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing damage from attacks made without advantage.

To give players another method to combat this and encourage adventurous thinking, the following variant condition can be utilised to help gain advantage:



***Climb Onto a Bigger Creature***
Using at least one free hand, a creature can use it's action (or an attack as part of the attack action) to attempt to climb on to a creature that is two or more size categories larger than it. The creature must make a Strength (Athletics) check or a Dexterity (Acrobatics) check contested by the target's Strength (Athletics) check or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check. On a success, the creature is *attached* to the target.



***Attached***
A creature that is Attached to an object or another creature is moved with that object or creature whenever it moves.  Any effect that could be used to Grapple a creature can be used to Attach to a creature too large for the Grappler to grapple.

**Holding On.** To remain attached the creature must be holding on to the target with at least one free hand, or lose the condition.

**Clambering.** The attached creature can move around within the larger creature's space, treating the space as difficult terrain.

**Advantage.** The attached creature has advantage on melee attack rolls against the target.

**Ending the Attached Condition.** A creature can use its action to attempt to break all creature's attachment to it. To do so, it must succeed on a Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check contested by each target's Strength (Athletics) or Dexterity (Acrobatics) check.

**Releasing an Attachment.** A creature that imposes the Attached condition can end that condition on its turn, with no action required.
